Tips for Success

Meal Prep

Cook protein in bulk on Sundays and portion out carbs. This makes daily meal assembly quick and keeps you on track when life gets busy.

Pre-Log Your Food

Log your meals in the morning so you know what you're working with. This prevents end-of-day scrambling and helps you make better choices throughout the day.

Workout First Thing

When possible, get your workout done before the day starts. It eliminates decision fatigue and ensures life doesn't get in the way.

Progress Photos

Take photos weekly at minimum. Same time, same pose, same lighting. Daily is better for data - you won't see changes day-to-day, but you'll have the timeline when you look back.

Find Your Flex Meal Rhythm

Maybe it's every Saturday dinner, maybe it's date night. Having a predictable pattern makes it easier to plan around and reduces the mental load of deciding when to use it.

Grace Days Are Strategic

Don't waste grace days on lazy days. Save them for when life genuinely throws you a curveball - sick kid, work emergency, travel. They're insurance, not get-out-of-jail-free cards.
